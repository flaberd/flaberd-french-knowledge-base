## 2. 101 French basics — general pronunciation rules

1. Stress always falls on the **last syllable** of a word (ex. *un cinéma* → [si.ne.ˈma]).
2. These letters are usually **silent** at the end of a word: **E, D, S, X, Z, P, T, G** (and combinations like *es, ts, ds, ps*).
   - Exception: **-é** is always pronounced (e.g. *la santé* → [san.te]).
3. **H** is never pronounced anywhere in French (it is used to form sounds like *ph*, *ch*).
4. Key rule: a final **-e** (feminine ending) "reactivates" (makes pronounced) the consonant before it.
   - *petit* [pə.ti] → *petite* [pə.tit]
   - *grand* [ɡʁɑ̃] → *grande* [ɡʁɑ̃d]

Mnemonic given in the lesson for the silent final consonants (**E D S X Z P T G**):

- English: *Every Dog Sits, Xylophones Play Zumba, Time Goes*
- Ukrainian (teacher's version): *(Е)тот (Д)ед (С)пит, (Х)отя (З)вон (П)угает (Т)русливых (Г)усей*

## 3. La lettre C (the letter C)

1. The combination **"ch"** gives the sound [ʃ] (like Ukrainian [ш]): *chat* [ʃa].
2. At the end of a word, C is usually **[k]**: *parc* [park]. It is silent after nasal vowels — *banc* [bɑ̃] — and in a few specific words (*porc* [pɔːʁ], *estomac* [ɛstɔma], *tabac* [taba]).
3. **"Ç"** always gives **[s]**.
4. C gives **[s]** before **i, e, y**: *cirque* [siʁk]. In all other cases C gives **[k]**: *cage* [kaːʒ].

Mnemonic: **(I)** I **(E)** eat **(Y)** yogurt — i.e. C softens to [s] before I, E, Y.

## 4. La lettre S (the letter S)

1. Between two vowels, S gives the sound **[z]**: *rose* [ʁoːz].
2. In all other cases, S gives **[s]**: *veste* [vɛst].
3. A double **"ss"** always gives **[s]**: *classe* [klas].
