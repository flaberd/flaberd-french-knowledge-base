# Basic Information

## French

mon (ma, mes)

## Part of Speech

Determiner

## Ukrainian

мій / моя / мої (присвійний прикметник)

## English

my (possessive adjective)

# Forms *(if applicable)*

| Subject | Masculine singular | Feminine singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|
| Je | mon père | ma mère | mes parents |
| Tu | ton père | ta mère | tes parents |
| Il/Elle/On | son père | sa mère | ses parents |
| Nous | notre père | notre mère | nos parents |
| Vous | votre père | votre mère | vos parents |
| Ils/Elles | leur père | leur mère | leurs parents |

---

# Pronunciation *(if applicable)*

- **mon** + vowel-initial noun → liaison in [n]: *mon_ami*, *mon_enfant*.
- **mes** + vowel-initial noun → liaison in [z]: *mes_amis*, *mes_enfants*.
- "Ma", "ta", "sa" become "mon", "ton", "son" before a feminine noun starting with a vowel: *ma amie* → **mon amie** ; *ta erreur* → **ton erreur**.

---

# Notes

The possessive adjective agrees with the noun it accompanies (the thing possessed), not with the possessor — unlike English ("his"/"her") or Ukrainian, which agree with the possessor. *Son père*, *sa mère*, *ses parents* can all mean either "his" or "her", depending only on context.
