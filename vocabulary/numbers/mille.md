# Notes

The source lists "un mille – a thousand." This is recorded as given, but it is flagged for clarification: in standard French, *mille* as the number "one thousand" is invariable and normally used without an indefinite article (e.g. *mille personnes* = "a thousand people," not *un mille personnes*). *Un mille* more commonly refers to "a mile" (a unit of distance) in standard usage. The lesson did not clarify this distinction, so both the source's translation and this ambiguity are preserved here rather than silently corrected.
