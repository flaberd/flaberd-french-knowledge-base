# Color adjectives: agreement and invariable colors

## Colors as nouns

*Une couleur* is feminine, but several colors also function as masculine nouns naming the color itself: **le blanc, le bleu, le rouge, le noir**.

- Le bleu est ma couleur préférée.

## Regular agreement

Most color adjectives agree in gender and number like ordinary adjectives:

- Un pantalon bleu → Une robe bleue
- Une robe blanche
- Un chapeau vert → Une pomme verte

## Colors that already end in unstressed -e (no separate feminine form)

Some colors (rouge, jaune) already end in -e, so the masculine and feminine forms are identical — this is the regular "-e ending" pattern, not a special exception:

- Une écharpe rouge (rouge ne change pas au féminin, comme jaune, jeune)

## True invariable colors

**Orange** and **marron** never change, in either gender or number — even in the plural, unlike regular adjectives:

- Des vestes **orange** (et non *oranges*)
- Des chaussures **marron** (et non *marronnes*)

*(Teacher's note: this distinction is not essential to memorize immediately — it is enough to recognize it.)*
