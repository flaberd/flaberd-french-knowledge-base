# Indefinite articles: un, une, des

## Forms

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| Masculine | un | des |
| Feminine | une | des |

## Use

The indefinite article designates a thing or person that is not specific, or is mentioned for the first time — a member of a category, or a quantity of one (un = 1).

- Masculine, person: un homme. Masculine, thing: un arbre.
- Feminine, person: une femme. Feminine, thing: une route.
- Plural (both genders): des arbres, des fleurs, des oiseaux.

Contrast with the definite article, used for a unique/specific thing or a generality — see [grammar/definite-articles.md](definite-articles.md).

## Pronunciation

- "un" + vowel-initial word → liaison in [n]: *un ami*, *un homme*.
- "des" + vowel-initial word → liaison in [z]: *des amis*, *des hommes*.
