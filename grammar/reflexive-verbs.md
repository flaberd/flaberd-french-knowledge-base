# Reflexive / pronominal verbs (les verbes pronominaux)

## Concept

When the subject of a verb is also its object, French uses a reflexive pronoun: "Je lave la voiture" (I wash the car) vs. "Je me lave" (I wash myself — "me" = moi-même). The infinitive of a reflexive verb is written with "se": laver → **se laver**.

"Dites: Je me regarde. Ne dites pas: Je regarde moi."

## Conjugation pattern (se laver)

| Pronom | Reflexive pronoun | SE LAVER |
|---|---|---|
| Je | me | je me lave |
| tu | te | tu te laves |
| il/elle/on | se | il se lave |
| nous | nous | nous nous lavons |
| vous | vous | vous vous lavez |
| ils/elles | se | ils se lavent |

The reflexive pronoun always agrees with the subject, and the verb itself still conjugates normally for its own group (se laver is a 1er groupe -er verb; see [grammar/first-group-verbs-present.md](first-group-verbs-present.md)).

## Elision

"Me" becomes "m'", "te" becomes "t'", "se" becomes "s'" before a vowel or a mute "h": je **m'**appelle, tu **t'**habilles, il **s'**ennuie, nous nous_amusons, vous vous_ennuyez.
