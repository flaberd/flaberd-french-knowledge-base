# Possessive adjectives: mon, ton, son...

## Key contrast with English

In French, the possessive adjective agrees with the noun it accompanies (the thing possessed), **not** with the possessor — unlike English, where the possessive agrees with the possessor's gender.

- le père, la mère, les parents **de Jean** → **son** père, **sa** mère, **ses** parents
- le père, la mère, les parents **de Marie** → **son** père, **sa** mère, **ses** parents

English: Jean → *his* father/mother/parents ; Marie → *her* father/mother/parents. French uses the same word "son/sa/ses" for both, because it agrees with père/mère/parents, not with Jean or Marie.

## Forms

The possessive varies with the person of the possessor, and with the gender/number of the noun possessed:

| Subject | Masculine singular | Feminine singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|
| Je | mon père | ma mère | mes parents |
| Tu | ton père | ta mère | tes parents |
| Il/Elle/On | son père | sa mère | ses parents |
| Nous | notre père | notre mère | nos parents |
| Vous | votre père | votre mère | vos parents |
| Ils/Elles | leur père | leur mère | leurs parents |

## Elision before a vowel

"Ma", "ta", "sa" become "mon", "ton", "son" before a feminine noun starting with a vowel (for pronunciation, avoiding hiatus):

- ma amie → **mon amie**
- ta erreur → **ton erreur**
- sa adresse → **son adresse**

*(Source note: the source PDF literally wrote "« as » devient « son »" for the third example, which is a typo for "sa" — preserved here as a documented correction rather than silently rewritten. See [lessons/lesson_00017.md](../lessons/lesson_00017.md).)*

## Pronunciation

- **mon** + vowel-initial noun → liaison in [n]: *mon_ami*, *mon_enfant*.
- **mes** + vowel-initial noun → liaison in [z]: *mes_amis*, *mes_enfants*.
