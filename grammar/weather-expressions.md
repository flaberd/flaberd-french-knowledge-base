# Weather expressions (la météo)

Weather in French is usually described with the impersonal subject **il**, not **c'est**.

- Il fait chaud. *(It's hot.)*
- Il fait froid. *(It's cold.)*
- Il fait beau. *(It's nice out.)*
- Il pleut. *(It's raining.)* — verb [pleuvoir](../vocabulary/verbs/group-3/pleuvoir.md)
- Il neige. *(It's snowing.)* — verb [neiger](../vocabulary/verbs/group-1/neiger.md)

## Nuance: "il fait" vs. "c'est"

> Dites : Il fait chaud. Ne dites pas : C'est chaud.

"Il fait chaud" describes the weather/ambient temperature; "c'est chaud" describes a specific object being hot to the touch (e.g. a plate). Confusing the two is flagged in the source as a common mistake.

## Temperature

Degrees are given with **il fait** + number + **degrés**: *Il fait vingt-cinq degrés.*
