# Les voyelles nasales (nasal vowels)

*No equivalent in Ukrainian or English.*

1. **"on"**, **"om"** → **[ɔ̃]**: *bon*, *monde*, *chanson*.
2. **"en"**, **"em"**, **"an"**, **"am"** → **[ɑ̃]**: *vent*, *temps*, *chambre*, *enfant*, *ensemble*.
3. **"in"**, **"im"**, **"ain"**, **"aim"**, **"ein"**, **"ym"**, **"yn"**, **"un"** → **[ɛ̃]**: *vin*, *simple*, *pain*, *faim*, *plein*, *un*, *matin*, *important*.
   - **"ien"** → **[jɛ̃]**: *chien*, *rien*, *canadien*.

## Denasalization

A nasal vowel loses its nasal quality (and is pronounced as a plain vowel + the regular consonant sound) when:

- the nasal consonant is **doubled** (**nn**, **mm**), or
- the nasal consonant is followed by **another vowel**.

| Word | Transcription | Nasal? | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| canadien | [ka.na.djɛ̃] | Yes | "ien" gives [jɛ̃] |
| canadienne | [ka.na.djɛn] | No | "n" + "e" → regular "n" |
| bon | [bɔ̃] | Yes | "on" gives [ɔ̃] |
| bonne | [bɔn] | No | "n" + "e" → regular "n" |
| ukrainien | [y.kʁɛ.njɛ̃] | Yes | "ien" gives [jɛ̃] |
| ukrainienne | [y.kʁɛ.njɛn] | No | "n" + "e" → regular "n" |
| important | [ɛ̃.pɔʁ.tɑ̃] | Yes | "n" + "t" → nasal stays (word-final) |
| importance | [ɛ̃.pɔʁ.tɑ̃s] | Yes | "an" gives [ɑ̃] |
| année | [a.ne] | No | "an" before a consonant *(as transcribed in the source)* |
| simple | [sɛ̃pl] | Yes | "im" before consonant "p" → [ɛ̃] |
| imaginer | [i.ma.ʒi.ne] | No | "im" before a vowel → regular "m" |
| impossible | [ɛ̃.pɔ.sibl] | Yes | "im" before consonant "p" → [ɛ̃] |

## Practice words

[ɔ̃]: Bon, Monde, Nom, Long, Chanson, Pont, Tombe, Salon

[ɑ̃]: Vent, Temps, Chambre, Enfant, Ensemble, Dent

[ɛ̃]: Vin, Simple, Pain, Faim, Plein, Un, Matin, Rien, Sympa, Brun

"ien" ([j]): Chien, Rien, Canadien, Musicien, Ancien, Italien, Pharmacien, Magicien, Parisien

Also: un, une, brun, brune, important, bien, bienne, année, canadien, canadienne, imagine

## Notes

- The source's explanation for *année* ("an before a consonant") appears inconsistent with its own table: *année* is split as *an-née*, so the "n" is actually followed by the vowel "é", not a consonant — matching the general denasalization-before-a-vowel rule above. Preserved as written in the source; not silently corrected.
- The source also poses an open question in lesson 5 ("What will happen if the nasal sound is before the vowel?") without giving a written answer; the *imaginer* / *année* examples above already illustrate the denasalization-before-a-vowel case.
